<p><p>Biodegradable paper and plastics packaging primarily includes polylactic acid (PLA) and polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA). PLA, derived from renewable resources like corn starch, is widely used for its compostable properties, making it suitable for food packaging and disposable items. PHA, produced by microbial fermentation of organic materials, offers versatility and biodegradability in diverse environments. Both types cater to the growing demand for sustainable packaging solutions, addressing environmental concerns while providing effective alternatives to traditional plastic materials.</p></p>
