<p><p>The biosafety autoclave market is categorized by biosafety levels (BSL) which define the containment precautions required for handling pathogens. BSL-1 autoclaves are designed for low-risk agents, suitable for basic laboratory work. BSL-2 autoclaves handle moderate-risk agents and require more stringent safety measures. BSL-3 autoclaves are used for highly infectious agents, necessitating advanced containment features. BSL-4 autoclaves cater to the highest risk pathogens, offering maximum protection and specialized containment systems, thereby ensuring safety in high-security research environments.</p></p>
